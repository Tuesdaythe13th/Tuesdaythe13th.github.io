As a finalist at the Stanford AIMI Symposium, this talk addressed critical gaps in how we evaluate AI systems intended for mental health applications. Standard benchmarks prioritize technical performance but often neglect psychological safety, cultural competence, and therapeutic alignmentâ€”factors essential for responsible deployment in mental health contexts.

The presentation introduced socio-affective benchmarking frameworks that:
- Center the experiences of underserved communities in evaluation design
- Integrate clinical expertise and lived experience into benchmark development
- Account for parasocial dynamics and attachment patterns in AI interactions
- Provide actionable metrics for therapeutic alignment and psychological safety

This work bridges AI safety research, clinical psychology, health equity, and community-engaged evaluation design, offering practical tools for organizations deploying AI in sensitive mental health applications. The frameworks inform ongoing development of the "DSM for AI Systems" and SCAI risk assessment methodologies.
